Chapati

Chapati Recipe

It is also known as roti, rotli, safati, shabaati, phulka, chapo, and roshi. It is a staple food in East Africa, India, Nepal, Bangladesh, Pakistan, Sri Lanka, Arabian Peninsula, and the Caribbean.

Chapati is an unleavened flat-bread originating from the Indian sub-continent.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups of flour
  • 1½ cups of warm water
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar
  • Cooking oil

Steps

  1. Add 3 cups of flour in a bowl
  2. Add salt, sugar, 2 tbsp of oil and 1½ cups of water in a separate jar/ bowl.
  3. Stir until the salt and sugar dissolves.
  4. Add the liquid mixture in step 2 in the flour bowl (step 1) and mix well.
  5. Keep kneading for 10 minutes and add flour if needed until the dough becomes non-sticky.
  6. Add 2-3 tbsp of oil and continue kneading until the oil mixes well and the dough is soft.
  7. Cover the dough and leave it for 40 minutes.
  8. After the 40 minutes, divide the dough into 10 - 15 equal parts making ball like shapes.
  9. Arrange them in a flat surface dusted with flour(Cover with a damp tablecloth to avoid drying).
  10. Dust flour in the flat place and take one of the balls and start rolling with a rolling pin to a circular shape.
  11. Brush oil on top and roll it inwards to form a shape-like a rope.
  12. Create a coil-like shape and press the ball down with your palm to make it flat.
  13. Repeat this process to the rest of the remaining balls of dough.
  14. Start rolling each of the coil-like shape doughs with the rolling pin to form a circular shape again.
  15. In a hot pan, place the rolled out circular chapati and fry each side with little oil until its golden brown on medium heat.
  16. Place your cooked chapati in a flat plat and cover with an aluminum foil.
  17. Repeat this step to the rest of the coil-like dough.
  18. Serve with your favorite stew
